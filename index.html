Chess is one of the world's oldest and most influential strategy games, with roots tracing back more than 1,500 years to the ancient Indian game chaturanga, which represented military divisions such as infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots. From India, the game spread to Persia, where it became known as shatranj, and later traveled through the Islamic world into Europe during the Middle Ages. Over centuries, the rules evolved, especially in 15th-century Europe, where powerful new moves for the queen and bishop transformed chess into the fast-paced game recognized today. Chess became associated with intellectual skill, patience, and strategic thinking, eventually growing into a global competitive sport with international tournaments and world champions such as Garry Kasparov and Magnus Carlsen. Today, chess is played both casually and professionally by millions of people worldwide, including through online platforms and artificial intelligence-driven training tools.
